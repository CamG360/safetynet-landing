The Critical Flaw

"Text Me When You're Safe" fails in silence.

The current system relies on you being able to act. If you go silent, the system breaks. SafetyNet was built to provide the safeguard for this exact scenario.

Founder Story

Campbell McCord

"I spent 20 years designing control systems that catch problems before they escalate. Now, I'm applying those same principles to the most important system of all: your personal safety."

Why I Built SafetyNet

In my work, silence triggers an alarm. If a critical system stops reporting, we know immediately that something is wrong. We don't wait for it to call for help.

"In everyday life, silence is just... silence. If you can't reach your phone to say 'I'm in trouble,' no one knows."

I built SafetyNet to close that gap. It brings the "silent safeguard" principle to personal safety. It protects you exactly when you can't protect yourself—triggered not by your action, but by your inaction.

The moments that defined the mission

We studied real scenarios where traditional SOS buttons failed.

The bad date

Stuck in an unsafe situation for hours. No one knew there was a problem because she couldn't text.

The compromised state

A friend's drink was spiked on a business trip. He became unconscious, so he couldn't ask for help.

The solo accident

A colleague slipped while hiking alone. No one knew she was in trouble until it was too late.

The false assumption

Friends held hostage in an Airbnb. Everyone assumed they were safe because they were "together."

The traveller's fear

"If I stopped posting on social media, how long would it take for anyone to actually notice?"

The missing context

Even when someone was reported missing, search teams had no starting point (Last Known Location).

The Realization

The pattern was always the same: Everyone assumed they were fine—until they weren't.

No signal. No context. No plan.
