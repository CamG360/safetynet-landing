02 / What they receive

Not a notification.
A plan of action.

When SafetyNet fires, your emergency contact gets a real SMS and email — with your plans, the time you missed, and exactly what to do, in order.

  • Plan context — who you're with, where you went
  • Location/venue — the address you shared
  • Step-by-step instructions — call you, then the venue, then 999

03 / What you might be wondering

The honest answers,
up front.

No, no, no, no. The four things you're already thinking.

No.

Am I being tracked?

SafetyNet never tracks your location in the background. The timer runs on our servers — your phone is never pinged.

No.

Are my contacts seeing my plans?

Your plan stays private — only revealed if an alert is triggered. Until then, no one sees where you are or who you're with.

No.

Will this drain my battery?

Monitoring runs entirely in the cloud. No background GPS. Your battery is untouched.

No.

Will my contacts get spam?

99% of the time, your contacts hear nothing. Only a real alert reaches them — with everything they need to act.

08 / Our story

The pattern was always
the same.

I kept coming across the same problem with "text me when you're safe." Everyone assumed they were fine — until they weren't. No signal. No context. No instructions.

This conflicted with the safeguards I used every day in business: dead-man's switches, monitored timeouts, automatic escalation. If a server can have one, why not a person walking home?

SafetyNet doesn't restrict your life — it enables it.

The moments that defined the mission

Real scenarios where "text me when you're safe" failed.

The bad date

Stuck in an unsafe situation for hours. No one knew there was a problem because she couldn't text.

The compromised state

A friend's drink was spiked on a business trip. He became unconscious and couldn't ask for help.

The solo accident

A colleague slipped while hiking alone. No one knew she was in trouble until it was too late.

The false assumption

Friends held hostage in an Airbnb. Everyone assumed they were safe because they were "together."

The traveller's fear

"If I stopped posting on social media, how long would it take for anyone to actually notice?"

The missing context

Even when someone was reported missing, search teams had no starting point.
