The moments that defined the mission

We studied real scenarios where traditional SOS buttons failed.

The bad date

Stuck in an unsafe situation for hours. No one knew there was a problem because she couldn't text.

The compromised state

A friend's drink was spiked on a business trip. He became unconscious, so he couldn't ask for help.

The solo accident

A colleague slipped while hiking alone. No one knew she was in trouble until it was too late.

The false assumption

Friends held hostage in an Airbnb. Everyone assumed they were safe because they were "together."

The traveller's fear

"If I stopped posting on social media, how long would it take for anyone to actually notice?"

The missing context

Even when someone was reported missing, search teams had no starting point (Last Known Location).

The Realization

The pattern was always the same: Everyone assumed they were fine—until they weren't.

No signal. No context. No plan.
